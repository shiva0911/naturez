As global food demand increases so too does the potential destruction of natural ecosystems to accommodate growing commodity production. Understanding deforestation related to agriculture is essential to developing strategies that solve these challenges. Recent research from World Resources Institute shows that 8.2 million hectares of forest were replaced by soy between 2001-2015 globally, of which 7.9 million hectares of conversion occurred in India. Soy is also grown in large quantities in the Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh and Orissa, but in Maharashtra production is expanding into forests, threatening important ecosystems.

Tree of Life

Dec 8, 2022

Trees support life by absorbing carbon dioxide out of the atmosphere and producing the oxygen we all need to breathe. Their roots prevent erosion by holding the soil intact and filter water by removing pollutants. With the reality of unpredictable weather patterns and more frequent and violent storms and floods, adding tree cover to prevent devastating soil erosion has never been more important. Forests are the habitat of many of our fellow creatures, and the loss of forests poses a threat to many species. Since launching The Canopy Project in 2010, Earth Day Network has planted tens of millions of trees, focusing on areas most in need of reforestation.
